Chapter 1 - Sundering Titan
"The rate at which work is achieved is halving each day. Estimates continue to fall outside of the projected zone. Severance is needed, but no one is bold enough to take responsibility. More importantly, no one outside of myself cares enough to take action. It’s like a continued fall to grace was the expected thing to happen."
Sabrina read the note loudly and couldn't help but nod in approval. To her, it sounded like corporate talk, maybe the words of a once important CEO before taking his own life. She thought: “Death and taxes will eventually reach all of us, in no particular order.” 
She let go of the note and watched as it slowly fell down to the ground in a rotating, predictable motion. She mumbled to herself: “My physics teacher would spend half an hour explaining why this happens.”
Sabrina continued to walk towards the northern exit of the building, stopping from time to time to admire how the light passing through the windows would suddenly bring dust particles into focus, exposing their dance in the still air of the afternoon. Then she would pick up another random piece of paper lying on the floor and proceed to read it out loud. Note after note after note, as she was getting closer to the exit.
Sabrina liked hearing her own voice. Pieces of random written information and walking through the empty halls of the building would encourage her to work on her diction, as she was generally too lazy to consider it otherwise.
Reaching the end of the hallway and looking outside, the scorching heat of the desert suddenly blasted her face, as if to remind her why she took refuge there to begin with. But there was no staying inside anymore. There was no more water to be found, so it didn’t make any difference if she would remain there or continue her trek to find her mentor, Hess. And if she were to die crossing a desert, then at least whoever would find her would have a good story to tell.
Stepping outside, save for a weird shadow coming from the north, there was nothing but sand. The calmness of the desert encompassed her completely. Sabrina took one last look at the abandoned palace she was leaving behind and decided to go in the direction of the shadow, as for the moment it was the closest thing she had to a compass.
A grueling two hours walk later, she arrived at the source of the shadow: a three-story tall statue of the river God Arno greeted her with its penumbra, as Sabrina laid down at its base to rest. 
“A river God in the desert. Now that’s a fitting joke.” she thought. Propping her back onto the stone behemoth, there was a loud crackling sound and suddenly the statue started splitting in half, revealing the entrance to an underground space. Sabrina jumped back up at the sound of the noise, facing the opening and the complete lack of light that it emanated. 
A river God statue in the desert. An underground access to “something”. The words of her mentor Hess continuing to echo in her head: “Find me, no matter what it takes.” Everything was there to simply show her the way.
“Guess we’re going down then.” Sabrina told herself as words of encouragement. As she put the first step into the darkness, the crackling sound appeared again and the two halves of the statue started merging back into a single entity, closing the entrance after her. Pitch black would soon embrace her. Then, Sabrina remembered that she didn’t carry a flashlight.
*

Hess had taken shelter in a cave system off the Nevada mountains in her search for Lucas, whom went to measure evolutive conditions of the Devils Hole and patch the inter-dimensional tear caused by the latest geo-storm. The blue pupfish that lived in the Devils Hole was to transform in a very unique brand of blue (male) or golden (female) dragon, which the Centre of Command refused to let happen. Lucas had gotten lost in his first mission in the realm when he tried to cross the Death Valley’s Funeral Mountains. He was top of his class and Sabrina’s partner, so Hess trusted him and Sabrina to resolve this task with ease given their training and the tech they had available to perform the task while also being monitored by the seniors. There was no beginner’s luck to this mission and things turned south pretty quickly.

The reddish atmosphere made it hard for Hess to move around between the peaks of Mount Rose, Rifle Peak, Snowflower Mountain and Slide Mountain with her bike. However, she managed to keep moving with a steady pace both underground and on the ground. Mapping out Lucas’s footprint was easy with the CRM system’s upgrade for geolocation. However, the storm and the geothermal activity in the area caused so much distort to the data, it was impossible to correlate it using satellites and extract him out. So Hess went out to find him, while Sabrina was assigned to do the reporting of the on-going mission in writing at the Centre.

With Pandora and Gaia out of the picture on intern recruiting, the model that was chosen to match the new comers in their live-long partnerships was a human personality model (that described the morals Zeus believed to have been ‘the nature of things’). This model looked at five different dimensions of personality: Extraversion, Agreeableness, Conscientiousness, Neuroticism and Openness to Experience. While Sabrina seemed to be a “factory settings” type of recruit, Lucas’s Openness to Experience was off the charts while lacking any Agreeableness index. What the two of them did have was a certain karmic alignment of synchronization that was only seen with in partnership of Lex and Hestia.

While walking through the cave system, Hess hears a loud thud - as if a door made of hard rock had closed nearby. With the rippled sound waves through the cave system, she is then quickly able to generate a location using the quantum calculator on her wrist and the scans available for the cave system. The loud noise came from somewhere close to her location, though she’d have to climb a bit to get there, it’s yet again one of those challenges she loves most given her favorite game is Tomb Raider. On she goes, just like Lara.

Close to the point of arrival, she softly calls out:

- Hello! Is somebody there?
- YES! YES! Master Hestia, is that you?
- Sabrina!? Jesus, what are you doing here? 
- Can we talk about it when we can see each other’s faces, please? I’m in a sea of darkness in here.
- Do you have anything to signal your location to me? A torchlight, a glow stick, some peanuts to throw around so I can find you? We’re too deep underground for me to use location tech on you.
- Will throwing pieces of paper work? I have some in the pockets of my backpack.
- Let’s give it a try and see where we get.

Sabrina starts feeling for the pockets of her backpack and starts pulling out some of the notes she’s been redacting earlier in the day. She starts by turning the first one into a small ball, then throws it straight ahead and hears it thump against the wall in front.

- Thank God I stopped moving! I can feel my head going against that wall now that I know it’s there. 
- What? I can’t see anything, Saber-Tooth. 
- Nothing, master. Hold up. I’m trying different angles.

One after the other, Sabrina kept throwing balls of paper around, but they weren’t making it anywhere close to Hess’s visible field, as they just didn’t fly far enough or simply hit the cave walls around. Sabrina then sighed. 

- I feel like I’m throwing these into a place with zero or too low of a gravity, Hess.
- I know, honey. Got something to bang noise into the walls next to you? I’ll use that to find you.
- Let me see what could ring against rocks enough for you to pick it up.

Sabrina then feels in her backpack pack for something appropriate. While slowly tossing and turning the contents, she starts to feel for something metallic in her belongings. Slowly losing her wit, Sabrina keeps mumbling and searching, then utters a loud ‘Aha!’.

- Did you find Thor’s hammer already?
- You bet I did! Lucas always laughs at me for bringing a hammer to the missions we have together. I thought I left it back at the Centre. 
- It was either that or an Excalibur joke, you know!? 
- Alright, allow me to start this party now.

Sabrina starts banging onto the rocks next to her with the hammer, which triggers enough sound waves in the lithophonic rocks around her to get picked up by Hestia’s location system. She was just above her, in something that resembled a shaft in the cave system.

- Alright, kiddo. Seems you are on top of me. We need to get you out of there now. I will get you something to light your path first, so what I need you to do is activate your CRM system and find where I am by looking down. 
- Activating system. I can see you now. 
- Next, I need you to move back about 3 steps, I see somewhat of an opening that can fit a flashlight. I will light it up so you can see it coming. You can follow my movements through the CRM interface.

Hestia manages to push the flashlight through the hole in the shaft. Sabrina lets out a squeaky sound, like she’d just opened her Christmas gifts on the 25th of December. 

- Now, I need you to go ahead, take two rights and come down to where I am. Let both CRM and the cave layout guide you. Remember! Two rights and then descend.

Hess and Sabrina reunite quickly after that. Sabrina was severely dehydrated, so Hess pulled a bottle of water and a candy bar to help her get some stamina back. The plan to find Lucas was to retrace his steps, which both were fairly familiar with. So Hess and her intern move towards the last known place in the dataset, the Death Valley.

- Now we can talk. 
- I was hoping we’d skip this part of me making excuses as to why I’m here. He’s my partner, I should be here. 
- I get the ‘should’, but the ‘need’ was more important. If he’s gone missing, it’s my duty to get him back, not yours. Yours is to write down what happened and learn from it, Saber.
- Grunt work is not for me. Nor is the desert - can we get the next assignment on a beach next time? Like you and Lex did?
- Me and Lex work city assignments. The beach report you read was an out of scope event. We didn’t even have our powers. I get you don’t like grunt work, but reading my missions should not be your focus, writing your own reports should be.

Both of them then sunk in silence as they walked through the dark and cold cave. In Hess’s mind, Lex was constantly making comments and giving out advice to “take it easy on her”, “remember how hard it was to be a newbie”, etc. She then looked at Sabrina and smiled calmly, as if she knew Lex was right behind them. After all, the ‘should’ and ‘need’ were just the same for their partnership - but there was an added ‘will’ that made them be recognized as the special ones.

Chapter 2 - Subterranean Schooner

Hess and Sabrina were walking for more than two hours already. The constant beeping of the CRM interface acted like a compass, allowing them to get closer to the Death Valley entrance. 

-	Should this path be that long and winding? asked and impatient and exhausted looking Sabrina.
-	No, we should have reached a fork about half hour ago, responded Hess, tapping on the CRM like it were a broken device that needs restarting. Something is wrong. But there’s no malfunction in the interface and the heading is correct. See? 4-2-0 North to North-West! We’re going in the right direction.
-	No malfunction that you know of. This is how every horror movie starts. I don’t want to end up like those women in “The Descent”.
-	What “Descent”?
-	You know what, let’s not talk about it anymore. I’m already visualizing the scenes and it’s not pleasant.

Sabrina’s gift of having a photographic memory was something that usually came in handy to her, but in this case, reliving a fictional terror scenario that scarred her as a child would be the worst that could happen as they were advancing through the caves. As it turned out, she didn’t need to focus on that thought too much. As they were reaching the final stretch of a long, winding tunnel, an unusual screech grabbed their attention.

-	Was that… a bat?
-	How do you know how bats sound like? asked a surprised Hestia.
-	There is this thing called research, you know? Back in your time I believe it was called “Googling”? Sabrina answered with a smirk.
-	 Don’t get cheeky with me. Best we see what that was about.

The two of them picked up the pace as the CRM beeping increased in frequency.

-	We’re close, Hestia declared with unyielding certainty.

A few moments later, the tunnel they were walking through gave way to an opening of massive proportions. Stepping into the clearing, Sabrina and Hess detected what looked like kilometers wide of shoreline and the biggest lake any one of them had ever come across until then, overground or underground. The walls of the tremendous underground cavern buzzed with activity from an inordinate number of deep cavern bats. 

-	Woah! Sabrina exclaimed with the euphoria of a child.
-	Woah is right, for once! Hess replied. But I’m pretty sure this is not the Death Valley.
-	Who cares what it’s called? It’s beautiful! 

Sabrina turned her head to the right and about one hundred meters away she noticed a weird contraption floating on the still water of the underground lake.

-	Is that a pirate boat??? Sabrina burst out.

Hess turned her gaze in the same direction as Sabrina. They both started walking, then the walk turned into running as Sabrina’s excitement was pushing her curiosity more and more. Reaching the shoreline, they got closer to the weird floating object that was seemingly just waiting for them to board it. Indeed, the object looked like a sailing boat. It had two giant masts, each with three rolled up sails, while the bow and hull were covered with a mixture of copper and gold ornaments. The old, wooden hull of the vessel was echoing creaking sounds across the vastness of the cavern, as the two explorers got closer and closer to it. Hestia tapped her CRM interface and turned the scanner towards the floating vessel. 

-	That, my dear, Hess said turning her head to a dumbfounded Sabrina, that I believe is called a “Subterranean Schooner”.
